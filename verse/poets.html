Katrine Stenbekk

Norwegian vocalist, songwriter

Imbuing verse with dynamic vocals, Stenbekk powers the melancholic Nordic folk sound of the band Kalandra. Her lyrics are as meaningful as her music is ethereal. Poems are meant to be performed, and she easily delivers some of the best performances.

Phil Kaye

Japanese-American poet, writer and filmmaker

Beyond using brilliant structure to amplify his messages, Kaye continues to actively work in bringing poetry to classrooms as the co-director of Project VOICE and previously to maximum security prisons as the head coordinator of Space in Prisons for the Arts and Creative Expression (SPACE).

Amanda Gorman

American poet, activist, and model

Gorman brings a touch of poetic voice to every piece she performs. Her delivery of the 2021 Presidential Inaugural address instantly placed her among my top poets. An rising icon, she also later appeared on the cover of TIME Magazine.

Featured: Kalandra's "The State of the World"

The State of the World

Released March 22, 2024 - Lyrics by Katrine Stenbekk, Jogeir Daae Mæland, Florian B. Winter, Tomás Cox

The state of the world has been on my mind
It's weighed on my shoulders, demanding my time
Something is missing, it's hard to stay sane
Why do the answers seem lost and arcane?

May war stop once and for all
And cast off the violence that has us enthralled
May healing not just be for the few
May peace be found for me and you

I speak of a message said thousands of times
Yet we smile when it's mentioned and roll our eyes
It may be naive, but I must believe
Inside every human, there's kindness still

Hope is a fire that burns in us all
If only an ember, awaiting your call
To rise up in triumph should we all unite
The spark for change is yours to ignite

I believe this message is worth our time
There are people who've suffered yet conquered the climb
Who've sat in the stillness through darkness and light
And found love in the colours between black and white

May compassion turn foe into friend
May self-awareness help us transcend
May forgiveness be given, despite lives that were lost
For isn't it peace all of us want?

The state of the world has been on my mind
Demanding my conscience and all of my time
Was there ever an age where it all made sense?
Or am I reminiscing about lost innocence?

Analysis

In Kalandra’s “The State of the World,” Katrine Stenbekk employs thought-provoking rhetorical questions and jarring diction to convey the confusion and moral complexity of the world around us today. She begins the song by stating that something feels wrong about the world and asks, “Why do the answers seem lost and arcane?” This sentiment reflects that of many disillusioned people today. Many people constantly witness corruption, war, and famine in the world and can only ponder the reason. By including this line, Stenbekk is able to connect with her audience while imploring them to keep those thoughts in their consciousness. She forces her audience to confront their own lack of answers.

In the same way, it is startling when she states “May war stop once and for all / And cast off the violence that has us enthralled.” The first part of that verse is commonly heard enough to be known as cliche, yet the second part is jarring: violence is not often thought of as drawing us in. By paradoxically stating such, Stenbekk forces us to reconsider our relationship with violence. She reveals an uncomfortable truth: violence trends, kindness does not.
